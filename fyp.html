Final Year Project · 2025 — ongoing

Objects for the Moment

A re-imagination of how Buddhist rituals, beliefs, and objects can be translated into contemporary interactions for the everyday. The project compresses these experiences into handheld devices, reinterpreted as modern-day dharanis—tangible interfaces that invite engagement through action and perception. Rather than replicating tradition, the work focuses on recreating the subtle, personal experience of the sacred in a reduced and accessible form for use in the moment.

Device 01

Sutra Scroller

A portable, handheld device for scrolling through the Diamond Sutra, character by character.
The Inspiration & Interaction The concept began from a parallel I noticed between karaoke and sutra chanting: both are guided performances where a participant follows a fixed text. I wanted to capture this experience in a portable format, allowing me to engage with the sutra at any time.
When thinking about the literal translation of karaoke—"empty orchestra"—I realized that a backing track is incomplete without the active engagement of the singer. The music only matters when someone steps up to participate. I took that philosophy and applied it directly to the hardware: the device remains "empty" and dormant until the user physically interacts with it. This requirement for bodily input perfectly mirrors the logic of the Tibetan Prayer Wheel, which must be physically spun to activate the merit of the prayers inside. Using this same interaction logic, I implemented a mechanical crank. As the user physically turns the handle, the device awakens, advancing the sutra character by character. The physical effort of cranking becomes the devotional act itself.

Device 02 · paused

KTV Sutra Device

A scrolling karaoke-style reader for the Diamond Sutra. Characters drift across a small ST7735 display while a wooden-fish knock sound marks each beat. Navigated with a rotary encoder, with an OLED minimap to keep your place.

Device 03 · exploration

Digital Amulet

A small wearable object — a digital take on the protective amulets you carry in a pocket. Currently being prototyped on a micro:bit V2 with a small OLED screen.
